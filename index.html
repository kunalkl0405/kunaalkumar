About Me

Kunal Kumar is a writer working at the intersection of thought and feeling — where language becomes a tool not just of expression, but of inquiry. Writing, for him, is less a craft and more a necessity: a way of understanding the world by passing it through words until something honest remains. His work moves across forms — poetry, editorials, and reflective prose — each demanding a different rhythm, a different patience, a different kind of truth.

In poetry, he explores silence, memory, and the quiet tensions of existence. In editorials, he engages with ideas, culture, and the shifting contours of public thought. Across all forms, there is a consistent pursuit: to bridge the distance between what is felt and what can be said.

His writing draws from both personal introspection and the broader social landscape, often residing in the space between the intimate and the universal.
